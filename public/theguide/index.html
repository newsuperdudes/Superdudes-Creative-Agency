04
Venue

Choose a Space That Becomes The Club

✓
Do

Choose venues with character — historic villas, blank-canvas warehouses, or atmospheric spaces that can be fully transformed into The Club identity.

✕
Don't

Avoid conference hotels and branded halls where permanent corporate décor conflicts with The Club's identity and cannot be concealed.

05
Design & Branding

Total Identity Takeover

✓
Do

Replace all signage, create a Google Maps point as "The Club", rebrand wayfinding, menus, coasters, and napkins. The venue must be unrecognizable.

✕
Don't

No competing sponsor roll-ups, no heavy single-use plastic constructions. When choosing between projection mapping and a plastic build — choose projection.

06
Creative & Entertainment

Build a World, Not an Activation

✓
Do

Curate immersive, original entertainment — live jazz, sketch artists, theatrical elements that dissolve into the atmosphere. Commit to the mystery.

✕
Don't

No generic cover bands. No LED-wall corporate stages. No confetti cannons. The Club is not a conference afterparty.

07
Food & Beverages

Every Bite Is Part of the Experience

✓
Do

Pre-test all catering with the actual team. Curate presentation — slate plates, branded menus, artisan stations. Beverages are part of the storytelling.

✕
Don't

No chafing dishes. No plastic cups. No generic hotel buffet lines. If it looks like a conference lunch break, it doesn't belong in The Club.

08
Staff & Service

Your Team Is the First Impression

✓
Do

Pre-screen and interview staff. Matching uniforms, full briefing on tone, exclusivity protocols, and guest interaction codes. Every team member must know the event programme and venue layout — ready to answer any guest question.

✕
Don't

No unbriefed, mismatched teams. If staff doesn't understand The Club's atmosphere, guests will feel it instantly.

09
Communication & Invites

Exclusivity Starts Before the Door

✓
Do

Physical key-card invitations with QR codes. Exclusive, personal tone. Every touchpoint says: you were chosen.

✕
Don't

No mass-appeal blasts. No "Everyone is welcome!" No clip-art newsletters. If the invite could be sent to anyone, it shouldn't be sent at all.

10
Technical Production

Sound & Light = Magic

✓
Do

Sound and light are two of the most powerful sensory tools available. Used right, they replace conventional décor entirely. Budget for specialists — this is where the magic lives.

✕
Don't

No visible cable spaghetti. No plastic podiums. No fluorescent ceiling strips. If the AV looks like a Tuesday morning conference call, it doesn't belong here.

11
Security & Access

The Door Is Part of the Experience

✓
Do

Security must be invisible and impeccably polite. Discreet guest verification via tablet. Personal greeting by name. The entrance should feel like arriving at a private residence — no visible security presence, just warmth.

✕
Don't

No queues. No clipboard lists. No "REGISTRATION A-F" paper signs. If guests wait in line with lanyards, the experience is dead before it starts.

12
Welcome Experience

First 90 Seconds Define Everything

✓
Do

Welcome cocktail, curated gift, personal host. The first moments should feel like a ritual — an unmistakable signal that this is not an ordinary event.

✕
Don't

No plastic badge tables. No tote bags of flyers. No "find your own seat." If the welcome feels like checking into a conference, the club identity is gone.

13
Signage & Wayfinding

Navigation Is Design, Not Information

✓
Do

Navigation is part of the space and the story. Make it intuitive, functional, and brand it when appropriate. Guests should never feel lost — but they shouldn't feel guided by a hotel concierge either.

✕
Don't

No A4 paper signs taped to walls. No mismatched fonts on easel boards. No over-designed luxury signage. If your wayfinding feels like a hotel lobby, you've missed the point.

14
Activities & Engagement

Analog Beats Digital. Always.

✓
Do

Analog, tactile, memorable. A vintage photo booth, hand-lettered cocktail menus, a live sketch artist. Activities that create physical artifacts guests take home — not screenshots they forget.

✕
Don't

No motion-tracking screens. No "wave your hand" activations. No QR-code scavenger hunts. If the engagement requires an instruction manual, it's already failed.

15
Content & Media

✓
📷
Vetted by Portfolio
& References
Do

Vet all videographers and photographers through strict portfolio screening. Send each creator a detailed creative brief well in advance.

✕
📱
Unvetted &
No Brief
Don't

No random photographers without references. No shooting without a creative direction document. Unguided content will dilute the brand.

16
Programme & Flow

Zero Dead Time. Ever.

✓
Continuous Flow
16:00 → Doors & first cocktail
16:15 → Immersive walkthrough begins
16:45 → Live performance drops in
17:10 → Shift: dinner stations open
18:30 → The "moment"
19:15 → Flow continues...
Do

Engage senses — love, passion, curiosity. Create a continuous flow where things happen simultaneously. Guests choose their own journey.

✕
Dead Zones
19:00 → Registration
19:30 → Wait for keynote
20:00 → CEO presentation
20:45 → Q&A panel
21:30 → "Free networking"
Don't

No corporate speeches. No "Dear colleagues, allow us to present." No dead time where guests feel abandoned or unsure what to do next.

17
Post-Event & Follow-Up

The Event Doesn't End at the Door

✓
Do

Cinematic recap video within 48 hours. Personal thank-you notes. A curated digital lookbook. Content so good, guests share it without being asked.

✕
Don't

No mass-email surveys. No stock-photo thank-you templates. No phone-quality dumps on social media. If the follow-up feels corporate, the magic is lost.
